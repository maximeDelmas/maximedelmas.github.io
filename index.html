About Me

I am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher at Idiap Research Institute, in the Neuro-symbolic AI Lab. My work focuses on Information Extraction, Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), Knowledge Graphs and Knowledge Discovery, applied to the Biomedical domain.

After a PhD at INRAE (Toulouse, France) focused on Knowledge Graph applications in metabolomics, I joined the Neuro-Symbolic Lab at Idiap (Martigny, Switzerland) in 2022. Combining Knowledge Representation with Natural Language Processing (Information Extraction, RAG), I had the opportunity to apply my research to complex real-world challenges (Antibiotic discovery, Climate risk analysis). Now, I explore how AI methods and Knowledge Graphs can push the boundaries of scientific discovery, especially in the biomedical domain.

I believe in curiosity and collaboration as the core of a healthy work environment. To me, both intense work and informal conversations over a hot coffee (or tea), are the essential ingredients for great projects and breakthrough ideas.

"The whole is greater than the sum of its parts." To me, this has a broad and beautiful meaning. Across scales, from a single cell to entire ecosystems, and to us as a scientific community, interconnected systems naturally generate emergent properties that often exist implicitly, waiting to be revealed. This perspective drives my research: uncovering latent knowledge emerging from the vast landscape of scientific literature.
