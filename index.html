About Me

I have long been fascinated by the idea that "the whole is greater than the sum of its parts." To me, this has a broad and beautiful meaning. From natural ecosystems to scientific communities, connected systems naturally give rise to something new: emergent properties that are often just waiting to be brought to light. This perspective drives my research: uncovering latent knowledge emerging from the vast landscape of scientific literature.

After a PhD at INRAE (Toulouse, France) focused on Knowledge Graph applications in metabolomics, I joined the Neuro-Symbolic Lab at Idiap (Martigny, Switzerland) in 2022. Combining Knowledge Representation with Natural Language Processing (Information Extraction, RAG), I have had the opportunity to apply my research to complex real-world challenges (Antibiotic discovery, Climate risk analysis). Today, I am deeply anchored in the biomedical domain, exploring how AI methods and Knowledge Graphs can push the boundaries of scientific discovery.

I believe in curiosity and collaboration as the core of a healthy work environment. To me, both intense, focused work and informal conversations over a hot coffee (or tea) are the essential ingredients for great projects and breakthrough ideas.
